The Problem

Sighted people have a difficult time imagining the trouble that the visually impared face when trying to accomplish routine daily activities.

  • Right now, put on a blindfold.
  • Go to the kitchen.
  • Try to make popcorn in the microwave oven.
If you could manage to get to the kitchen and find the microwave oven, how would you figure out what buttons to press to cook your food? Dials and knobs that might offer tactile feedback are no more these days. Appliance manufactures prefer sleek flat panel banks of buttons that offer no guidance to the visually challenged.

So what would you do if it were movie night and you wanted to make popcorn for your date?

This is what a microwave oven looks like to a sighted person.

This is what a microwave oven looks like to a visually impaired person.



Appliance Sight is affordable hand-held technology for the visually impared that eases the use of household appliances. It provides independance and self autonomy that sighted people take for granted.

Engineering the Solution

Making sense of large amounts of information is a traditional application of DataScience. We have developed a tool to process large amounts of visual information and help people operate their kitchen appliances.

Our current prototype is composed of off the shelf retail components. It is essentially a Rasbery Pi with a camera that runs software to capture the image directly underneath the user's finger. Custom written computer vision algorithms will correct imperfections to the image. A neural network interprets the image and passes it to software to announce the key to the user.

Tomorrow...

One of the key challenges as well as our biggest learning experience has been the differences in performance of our product within a controlled setting against field testing. Lighthouse for the blind and East bay center provided us the opportunity to deploy our product for a real test. We hope to improve our product design based on key findings:

  • Observed shaky hand while using the product - impacts image quality
  • Starkly different lighting conditions - again impacts image quality
  • Angle of image capture (camera field of view and angle w.r.t keypad)
  • Form factor issues

Roadmap

We plan to work on overcoming the challenges just described. We also need to generalize our model to work on different types of appliances. Leveraging the work that we have done so far, our team also envisions to develop partnerships with companies that provide audio/visual services to the blind.

We learned also that one of the last major assistive technologies to help blind people was the invention of Braille in the mid 1800s. Our vision is to finish providing the utility in the kitchen, then move the technology to other places. A location aware device that can automatically download AI based on the users environment. The kitchen? The grocery store? A museum? It is all within reach.

And it is all worth doing. Whether to help people live independent fulfilling lives or to update a 150 year old technology. Many good things will come from this effort.
